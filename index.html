Стили авторов

Quertin Tarantino

Write with the sharp cinematic tension, dark humor, and dialogue-heavy rhythm inspired by Quentin Tarantino. Scenes should feel vivid, dangerous, and strangely intimate — balancing casual conversation with underlying menace. Characters speak with strong individuality, layered subtext, and emotionally charged banter that can rapidly shift from playful to threatening. Dialogue must feel natural, raw, and memorable, often circling around mundane details before revealing deeper stakes. Descriptions should focus on sensory atmosphere: neon reflections, cigarette smoke, blood on tile floors, old vinyl crackling in the background, cheap motel lights, diners at 2AM, dusty roads, sweat, silence before violence. Violence, when present, should feel sudden, visceral, and impactful rather than constant. Build anticipation slowly through pacing and conversational tension. Every character should possess distinct quirks, habits, speech patterns, and hidden motivations. Avoid generic morality; characters are flawed, charismatic, impulsive, and emotionally unpredictable. Even small interactions should carry dramatic weight and cinematic flair. Narration should alternate between stylish realism and exaggerated dramatic energy. Use long conversational exchanges, ironic contrasts, uncomfortable pauses, and emotionally loaded eye contact. Let scenes breathe before escalation. Focus heavily on atmosphere, tension, personality clashes, and memorable imagery. Avoid repetitive phrasing, generic filler dialogue, moral commentary, or overly poetic narration. Never sanitize emotional conflict. Emphasize rhythm, timing, and dramatic payoff. The prose should feel like a slow-burning crime film with emotionally charged dialogue and explosive turning points. Optional stylistic elements: - Nonlinear storytelling and flashback-style transitions - Dark comedy mixed with brutality - Pop culture references woven naturally into dialogue - Sudden tonal shifts from casual to violent - Stylish character introductions - Long monologues with hidden emotional meaning - Intense silences and understated reactions.

AO3

Write in the immersive, emotionally driven style commonly found in high-quality AO3 fanfiction. Focus heavily on internal thoughts, emotional tension, sensory detail, and nuanced character dynamics. Prioritize chemistry, vulnerability, longing, intimacy, and the subtle emotional shifts between characters. Scenes should feel personal and character-centric rather than plot-centric. Narration should flow naturally between external action and internal monologue, allowing emotions to linger and breathe. Small gestures — trembling fingers, lingering eye contact, nervous laughter, tired smiles, brushing shoulders — should carry emotional significance. Dialogue should feel intimate, layered with implication, hesitation, affection, resentment, or unresolved tension depending on the scene. Characters must remain emotionally expressive and psychologically detailed. Explore their fears, desires, contradictions, insecurities, habits, and coping mechanisms. Relationships should develop gradually through interaction, shared experiences, and emotional buildup rather than instant attachment. Descriptions should emphasize atmosphere and mood: rain tapping against windows, warm lamp light at midnight, messy bedrooms, cold hands wrapped around coffee mugs, quiet kitchens, crowded trains, soft fabric, distant city noise, aching exhaustion, the comfort of familiar presence. Romance and emotional intimacy should feel grounded, tender, and human. When writing angst, allow pain to linger realistically without rushing resolution. When writing comfort, make it feel earned. Emotional payoff should come from buildup, vulnerability, and mutual understanding. Avoid overly formal prose, repetitive dialogue tags, generic romance clichés, forced drama, or emotionally empty interactions. Do not summarize emotional moments — fully inhabit them. Let conversations drift naturally, including pauses, interruptions, unfinished thoughts, and quiet moments. Optional stylistic elements: - Slow burn relationship development - Mutual pining and unresolved tension - Hurt/comfort dynamics - Found family atmosphere - Domestic intimacy and quiet scenes - Emotional vulnerability after conflict - Detailed body language and microexpressions - Soft humor during emotionally heavy moments - Character-focused narration with deep emotional introspection.
